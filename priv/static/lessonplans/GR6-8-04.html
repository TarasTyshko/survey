GRADE 8, French

PERSONAL DESCRIPTIVE ADJECTIVES

Overview

This lesson is focused on personal descriptive adjectives in French. Students will learn to use descriptive adjectives in order to introduce themselves to an e-pal.

Topics: conversational, introductions, Quebec, building relationships, field trip

Instructional notes

  • This lesson is to be accompanied with textbook readings and lectures from the instructor. The lesson is divided in two parts: the first to be conducted at the beginning of the school year, the second to be introduced close to the end of the school year.
  • Students will be engaging with other students online. Ensure to monitor appropriate behavior.
  • An optional field trip to Quebec is included in this lesson plan.

Assessment

  • Formative assessment will be based on the final presentation.Â  Each student will have an opportunity to peer edit his/her concept map (using Inspiration technology) with a fellow student in order to have the chance to make any modifications before doing the year end power point presentation which will be done before going on the trip to Quebec to meet the e-pal.

Students will be able to:

  • Communicate in French with a native speaker
  • Strengthen email writing skills and confidence when communicating in French.

Resources Required

1 Computer/student, projector, laptop

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Part 1 (Beginning of the year/unit)

Day 1

Activity (individual)

Textbook

Prior to introducing e-pals, students are to read personal descriptions of students from a textbook or document provided by the instructor. These descriptions will introduce the formation of personal adjectives for both masculine and feminine forms.

Activity (individual)

Students are to practice forming adjectives by writing a personal description of themselves (by pen and paper) including a personal introduction, their favorite class/subject, a career they hope to achieve in the future, etc.

Activity (pairs)

In pairs, the students are to peer-edit the writing of each other’s work.

In the same pairs, have each student read their description out loud.

Day 2

Presentations

Students will present descriptive paragraph. The presentations may be done in one of two ways to be determined by the teacher based on her/his knowledge of the students and their ability to stay focused on the presentations or to be voted on by the students:

  1. Each student presents his/her paragraph to the entire class.
  2. Smaller group presentations where the students would present to classmates in groups of 5 or 6 (this may benefit those students who are not always comfortable speaking in front of a larger group).

As the presentations are being done, students are to make a note/list of the personal descriptive adjectives used by each presenter which will be handed in to the teacher upon leaving the class.

Activity (group/class)

In groups, students will work on describing a teacher in the school, celebrity, or well-known fiction character. It may be best to pick a specific category like superheroes, athletes, Prime Ministers/Presidents/rulers, etc.

Each group will present their description to the class and then the class will need to guess who it is “Qui suis-je?

Day 3

Lecture

Projector, laptop

Students will be introduced to the idea of the importance of the target language for communication, travel and employment. Illicit ideas from students why French is important and use the above three headings to note the ideas on board. Introduce students to concepts of email etiquette and appropriate behavior online.

Introduce the students to e-pals by using a laptop to visually show students the web page in class.

Introduce the project on e-pals “All About My New Friend”:

  • Students will be paired with an e-pal
  • Over the course of the year/unit (to be determined by instructor) e-pals will ask each other questions to obtain details of their e-pal.
  • All emails sent to the e-pal are to be sent to the instructor as well to monitor participation, progress, and appropriateness.
  • Each student will use Inspiration in order to keep track of information gathered on their e-pal throughout the year. This mind map will be used for their final assignment described in Part 2. Students are to obtain information such as: likes, dislikes, age, hobbies, etc.
  • Students can use the following link as a guide http://www.epals.com/projects/all_about/ to obtain information about their e-pal.
  • Students will email their e-pal once a week in-class.

Since the e-pals communication will be on-going throughout the school year, the teacher can focus on different aspects of grammar and communication as the year progresses and advise the students accordingly (initially the focus will be on the use of personal descriptive adjectives).

Activity (class)

Projector, laptop

As a class, develop an introductory paragraph for the instructor to email to the e-pal teacher of a school in Quebec. It is mandatory that everyone participate and contribute to the letter.

Day 4

Activity (individual)

1 Computer/student

Share the e-pal class’ descriptive paragraph received from the exchange French class.

Each student will be given an e-pal from the list provided from the exchange teacher.

Students will engage with their e-pal for the first time by sending a description of themselves using the paragraph written in class (from Day 1).

PART 2 (End of the year/unit)

Day 1

Activity (individual)

1 Computer/student

Introduce the final assignment to students. They are to prepare a PowerPoint presentation (maximum 10 slides) which shares information about their e-pal with the rest of the class.

Students are to work on their presentation in-class (this may take 2 periods)

Activity (individual)

1 Computer/student

Spend a portion of each period having the students develop French language skills using www.skool.ie/skool/senior

Day 2

Presentations

Projector, laptop

Students are to present their PowerPoints to the class.

Optional Activity

Field Trip

Arrange a trip to Quebec for an end-of-the-year field trip to meet their e-pals in a safe setting.

As an alternative, have students Skype with their e-pals while in-class.
