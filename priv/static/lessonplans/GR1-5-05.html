Duration: 8 days

GRADE 5, French

Quebec and Quebecois Culture

Overview

This lesson plan introduces francophone culture, specifically Franco-Quebecois culture while integrating grammar, reading, and writing. The lesson provides authentic sources and opportunities for interactive (written and oral) communication in French.

Topics: Quebec, francophone culture, conversational, communication, oral French, written French

Instructional notes

  • All components of the projects are to be done in class to ensure students have access to help.
  • This lesson is intended to lead directly into the next Unit, “Carnaval de Bonhomme”.
  • This lesson plan includes the participation of a sister-school in Quebec via Skye chat to be arranged prior to the unit commencing.

Assessment

  • Students will engage in a 2-person team project which will require research and oral communication. Students will present their project to a sister-school in Quebec participating in a similar project about your region.

Students will be able to:

  • Understand and appreciate Franco-Quebec culture
  • Communicate orally effectively with Francophone peers.

Resources Required

Computer lab (or 1 Computer/student), projector, laptop

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1: Project Introduction

Discussion (class)

Projector, laptop

Show a YouTube video of Francophone culture images and ask students what they recognized as symbols of francophone culture - specifically referencing Quebecois culture. Students have been in French class since Grade 4, and last year they explored culturally relevant symbols/icons of "Francophonie"

Activity (individual)

Worksheets

Have students complete a KWL Sheet: “What I already know about Quebec/What I would like to know about Quebec” (the third question, “What I learned about Quebec” is to be completed after the 3rd day).

Assignment Instructions

SMART Board (or Projector)

Explain the scope and timeline of the project and the culminating task of presenting via Skype to a Francophone classroom.

Describe the team project and the 7 possible research topics/entertaining other topics for research and that PowerPoint technology will be addressed in the lab.

  • A 2-person team project incorporating grammar, reading, writing, oral comprehension and production into a PowerPoint presentation on their chosen area of research about Quebec or Quebecois culture: Sports, Entertainment, Foods, Monuments/buildings/cities, Myths and legends, History, or Natural phenomenon.
  • Describe the research technology - Pinterest - showing a class Pinterest page on the SMART Board

 

Computer work (pairs)

1 Computer/group

Divide the class into pairs and allow them a few minutes to choose their research topic. Allow them some time on the computers to browse the Teacher created Pinterest boards.

Create a class list of research teams and topics.

 

Day 2

 

Computer work (individual)

SMART Board, 1 Computer/student

Outline the success criteria for the project and create a project outline with the students on the SMART Board and post it to a class website, under project.

  • Team project must include: 5-7 slides, 1 minute oral presentation, 5-7 descriptive sentences. 

Review the specific grammar knowledge requirements for any written and oral portion of their presentation, verb tense and subject verb agreement.

  • Have them go to http://www.brainpop.fr/ to review their required grammar knowledge. 
  • Click on 'Francais', then 'Grammaire', then 'accord sujet-verbe'.
  • After viewing the short video clip, take the online test.

 

Show the students how to access the teacher-created Pinterest board for their research. Allow the students to explore all the pages under their specific interest topic.

Students are to start gathering and recording the information for their presentation in their word documents.

Students can use the Larousse online dictionary to translate any words they do not know in French (http://www.larousse.fr/dictionnaires/anglais-francais).

Day 3

Computer Work (group)

Projector, laptop, 1 Computer/group

Show students a PowerPoint presentation from the previous year (or own example).

Remind them of the success criteria for their presentation, post the criteria in the classroom.

Allow students time to plan out the sequence of their slides and write the descriptive sentences for the accompanying oral presentation which they can then type out using the classroom computer and save it in their word file for Day 4.

Ask students to complete the first part of the "What I learned about Quebec?" portion of their KWL from the first class.

Day 4

Discussion (class)

SMART Board (or Projector and laptop)

Allow students to finish typing in their sentences into their word document and have them email or link them to the instructor.

Pull up all the word documents on the SMART Board and open each file. Remind students that the goal is not to criticize the entries but to work on consolidating our grammatical knowledge as well as correcting our peer’s sentences.

The goal is for the whole class to help correct any spelling or grammar errors in the sentences.

Each sentence is pulled up and examined for agreement in gender and number between the article, noun, adjective and verb. Correct spelling of the nouns. Correct verb conjugation.

Day 5 & 6

Computer work (group)

1 Computer/group

Students will be using these two lab times to create their PowerPoint presentations, using their corrected sentences. They must be reminded that there is an oral component to this task and to practice reading their sentences which will not be visible on the PowerPoint display that the class is seeing.

The only research tools available to them are the specific websites that the Pinterest Board refers them to. It is important to remind them that these are the only websites that you expect to see open on their computer.

Day 7

Presentation (Run-Through)

SMART Board (or Projector and laptop)

Dry run for Day 8 presentation to our sister class.

Upload all of the presentations onto the Smartboard.

Review the protocol for peer presentations and how to ask questions: Comment, Ou, Qui, Est-ce-que, etc...

Each team has 1 minute for their presentation and 1 minute to answer questions from their classmates.

Decide as a class the order of the presentations for tomorrow's presentation to the sister school.

Have students complete the task for KWL sheet –“What I learned”

Day 8

Presentations via Skype

Projector and laptop

Be sure to have pre-arranged a time and date with the sister school and to confirm the day before. Confirm Skype connection.

Have students exchange polite pleasantries with their sister classroom: Bonjour, comment allez-vous? etc... 

Presentation of the student created PowerPoints begin using screen share on Skype with the sister school. Each team presents their research on their PowerPoint.

  • Sister school can ask questions to the students about what they learned, what they enjoyed about their research etc…
  • Thank you's and look forward to hearing our sister school's presentations about Toronto next week.

Consolidation. Ask the students what they enjoyed about this project, what they found challenging, what they learned that they did not know.
