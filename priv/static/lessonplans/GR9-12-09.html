Duration: 6 weeks

GRADE 9, English

Romeo and Juliet

Overview

This lesson will allow students to do a close reading of Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet through text dramatization, passage analysis, and character analysis. By incorporating video, online, and social technologies into the learning process, this lesson aims to break down the challenging language of Shakespeare and make it more accessible and enjoyable for students.

Topics: Shakespeare, drama, production, modern, character analysis, plot development

Instructional notes

  • Create a master list at the beginning of the unit to inform each student the date, scene, and character they will be performing as (assign a daily videographer as well)
  • A certain amount of time will be allotted each day for travel and set-up (to and from computer lab)
  •  Strive to encourage discussion around the text, and try to place those students who are struggling with   those students who have a good understanding when choosing groups.

Assessment

This unit will cover many areas of the Grade 9 Academic Curriculum, particularly in the following areas:

  • Oral Communication (Listening to Understand, Speaking to Communicate)
  • Reading and Literature Studies (Reading for Meaning, Understanding Form and Style, Reading With Fluency)
  • Writing (Developing and Organizing Content, Using Knowledge of Form and Style, Applying Knowledge of Conventions)
  • Media Studies (Understanding Media Texts, Understanding Media Forms, Conventions and Techniques, Creating Media Texts)

Students will be able to:

  • Translate and understand Shakespearian literature
  • Critically analyze and discuss themes, plot, and characters

Assignment

Introduce Shakespeare in Today’s World to class. This activity will commence for Act 3, Scene 5. Post the sign-up sheet so students can start signing up for the scenes they wish to perform. This assignment will have students perform in groups (numbers vary depending on number of characters in scene). This activity for the remainder of Romeo and Juliet is a dramatic reading of the scene they’ve signed up for—done in modern times. The students can be characters of any kind and can perform in any manner (setting can be changed). The only thing that remains the same is the language-- it cannot be changed, though sound effects or minor words/phrases can be added. Students must use cue cards instead of the text. The students’ individual performances will be peer reviewed. We will take this into consideration when giving each student a mark for Knowledge/Understanding.
