Duration: 8 days

GRADE 10, DESIGN

Architectural DesigN

Overview

This lesson introduces students to architectural design, interior design, and landscape design. Through creatively designing a bungalow, students will study house layout, building materials, furniture, and landscape components. Awareness of home design criteria will be addressed through research.

Topics: consumer product design, architectural design, urban planning, mechanical design

Instructional notes

  • This lesson is to occur in the second half of a design/technology course so students have been exposed to the design process of SPICE, SketchUp modeling fundamentals, and peer reviewing.

Assessment

  • Teacher would make anecdotal records throughout the design process based on teacher student interaction.  During the design process the student will be required to perform a number of hands on activities using the design software SketchUp.  Notes would be taken on the students abilities to apply the design process and follow the design criteria to develop the design solution.  The students will be required to think critically of peers’ designs and give feedback in areas that they need to consider in their design.

Students will be able to:

  • Identify user needs related to specified design criteria
  • Describe the process used to develop products
  • Use SketchUp to enhance understanding of architectural design
  • Build effective models and prototypes
  • Illustrate ideas and solutions using hand-drawn and computer illustrations, as well as technical drawings

Resources Required

Projector, laptop, 1 Computer/student, SketchUp® software

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1: Project Introduction and Sketch Room Layout

Lecture

Projector and laptop

Introduce the project with a recall exercise of the design process using a PowerPoint presentation.

Review the S.P.I.C.E. model (Situation/Scenario, Problem/Possibilities, Investigation/Ideas, Choose/Create, and Evaluate) and how it applies to this project.

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

Introduce the project criteria that will include the lot size and maximum coverage as defined by the city and the minimum room sizes as outlined by the building code for their bungalow.

Students are to use the SketchUp software to lay out their rooms, hallways, garage and general house layout:

While the students design their bungalow room layout, the teacher will act as a facilitator during this exercise and assist students when necessary.

Once a room layout plan has been complete by a student, the student will consult with the teacher and discuss how they have designed their bungalow creatively but still worked within the criteria of the project.

Day 2: Wall, Door, and Window Layout

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

The students will research building wall sizes for both interior and exterior wall standards. Window and door size standards will also be review and the students will use the software to experiment with their position in the house design. The students’ design will then be reviewed by the teacher for feedback to review the overall design layout before it is to be modeled in three dimensions.

Day 3: 3D Model of Walls, Doors, Windows, and Roof

Discussion (class)

Begin the lesson by having a class discussion about the construction materials and finishes to be used for the interior of the house that would include carpet, wood flooring, paint and wallpaper.

Discuss the construction materials for the exterior features that can include bricks, stone and glass windows.

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

The class will also use the Google warehouse to download window and door components for the house. The students will then use the software to model the walls in 3D and then proceed to add texture to the surfaces that represent the material used in the house design.

Day 4: Interior Design

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

Students will begin the class by doing some research on interior design. The students will use the internet to research the furniture makeup of the various types of rooms. They will then access the Google warehouse to download furniture for their house. The software gives the students the flexibility to both select furniture they desire and position it in the desired area of the house:

Day 5: Landscape Design

Discussion (class)

Projector, laptop

The teacher will demonstrate a slide show of different landscape components to the class using the LCD projector.

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

The students will then discuss aspect of the landscape design and apply it to their architectural design. The students can use their landscape knowledge and the online Google warehouse to download landscape features and place them in desired locations on the lot.

Day 6: Fly Through design, Peer review, and Modify design

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

Students will develop a virtual tour of their bungalow design. The students will use the SketchUp software to complete a fly-through of their design. After the fly-through is complete, they can then post their design online in the Google warehouse for peer to review.

Peer Review

Students and teacher will collaboratively develop a peer review form to critique their classmate’s bungalow designs. Each student will randomly select two classmates’ designs to perform the peer review on. The students will then submit their peer review to both the teacher and the classmate for review. Once the student receives their reviews they are given time to make improvements to their design based on the feedback.

Students will be given time to develop a PowerPoint presentation for their design.

Day 7: PowerPoint Presentation

Computer Work (individual)

1 Computer/student

Students will use the PowerPoint software to present their design project to the class. The students will use the S.P.I.C.E model to present the steps they took to complete the design.

Students will present the design challenges they faced and the strategies they used to overcome them, and discuss what things they might do differently and what they would change in their design if they had to do it again.

Day 8: Create a Community

Computer Work (class)

1 Computer/student

As a post design activity the class would create a community.

Combine all of the student’s bungalow designs together and create a neighbourhood. This could lead into an urban design unit about components of communities and the various facilities to make them function effectively. Google Earth could be also used as a mindtool in this unit to post street designs or provide a resource to students to research urban layouts.
