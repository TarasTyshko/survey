Quadratic Functions in the Vertex Form

Overview

This lesson plan focuses on quadratic functions.  These functions, in the vertex form, are used to understand the concept of parabolic relations and how transformations and translations affect the shape and positioning of the parabola.  The vertex form, y=a(x-h)2+k, will be broken down into its components and each component will be introduced individually. The class will explore what types of situations produce parabolic functions and look at parabolas that are seen in everyday life.

Topics: quadratic functions, vertex form, transformations, translations, coefficient, vertical shift, horizontal shift

Instructional notes

  • All worksheets and homework questions are included in the lesson plan Appendices.

Assessment

  • Assessment of student’s learning will be incorporated into each day and outlined in the lesson plans using current terminology as follows: assessment for learning (AforL), assessment as learning (AasL), and assessment of learning (AofL).

Students will be able to:

  • Relate transformations of the y=x2 graph to the algebraic equation y=a(x-h)2+k.
  • Understand the circumstances in which technology would be used over a paper-and-pencil approach.
  • Develop the ability to select appropriate technologies to perform assigned tasks

Day 1: Quadratic Relations in the Vertex Form

Students gain an understanding of the construction of parabolas, explore the differences between linear relations and quadratic relations, and explore the vertical stretch on the graph of y=ax2 and investigate the effect of a.

Discussion (Class)

SMART board

Review linear relationships (y = mx + b) and some applications of linear relationships (constant velocity vs. time, distance vs. time, etc.). Review what happens to numbers in general when their values are squared. Introduce the basic quadratic relation of y = x2. Discuss with the students what they believe the graph is going to look like. Is there anything that they see in the real world that is displayed by using a parabola? Show YouTube video “Who Cares About Parabolas” and some photos of parabolas integrated into Geometer Sketchpad ® on the SMART board.

Introduce the students to the terminology used in parabolic representation – vertex, axis of symmetry, minimum, maximum, intercepts. 

Ask: What will happen to the graph if you have a coefficient in front of the x2 so that the equation looks like y = ax2?

Lecture

SMART board, Day 1 Homework sheets

Summarize the role of a in the transformations (i.e. vertical stretch & compression) from findings:

When a is greater than 1, then parabola has a vertical stretch by a factor of a; When a is between the value of 0 and 1, then parabola has a compression by a factor of a; Also, when a is less than zero (i.e. a is a negative value) then the parabola is opening down

Day 3: Transformation of Quadratic Relation

Discussion (Class)

SMART board

Review the role of a and k in the algebraic representation of y = ax2 + k by showing the changing the a and k values in the SMART board with interactive simulations obtained from the internet by introducing the class to the following interactive simulations from the internet:

http://www.heymath.com/main/samples/us21/teacherstemplate.html

Demonstrate using the above website and discuss with whole class that a is vertical stretch if:

a >1 vertical expansion by factor of a and if 0 < a < 1 vertical compression by factor of a; And a < -1 reflection on x-axis and vertical expansion by factor of a and if -1 < a < 0 reflection on x-axis and vertical compression by factor of a. In addition, k is the vertical translation (shift); if k > 0 upward by k units and if k < 0 downward by k units

Introduce to students the role of h in the algebraic representation of y = (x –h)2 by demonstrating that h a horizontal translation using interactive simulation in the above mentioned website.
