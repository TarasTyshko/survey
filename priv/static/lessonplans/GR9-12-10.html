Duration: 8 days

GRADE 9, French

Le PASSÉ ComposÉ

Overview

This lesson is designed to teach students le passé composé (past tense) in French, integrating verbs (regular & irregular) and specific vocabulary. Students will use mobile devices, group work, and interactive computer activities to engage with the unit material.

Topics: interactive, passé compose, avoir, être

Instructional notes

  • BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) classroom environment used in this lesson.
  • Ensure that all applications and software are downloaded to student devices prior to beginning the lesson.
  • Day 2 requires a Google Doc to be pre-set up with various links to student-friendly resources and websites.

Assessment

  • Throughout the assessment process, opportunities will be given for students for self- and peer-assessment. Also, each assessment builds on the other, allowing the student to have a holistic and rounded learning experience before submitting the culminating assignment.

Students will be able to:

  • Form, understand, and use simple sentences in the past tense
  • Create collaborative media to enhance understanding
  • acquire knowledge and skills related to safe use of technology that they can transpose to other subjects

Resources Required

BYOD, Socrativesoftware, Larousse mobile app, Nearpod App access, Edmodo access, 1 Computer/student

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1: Introduction

Activity (individual)

BYOD

Have students bring their device to class and make sure there are additional devices available for students who do not have access to their own.

Students will complete a Socrative quiz to assess how familiar they are with le passé composé.

Lecture and Practice

BYOD

Direct the students to the Nearpod app where they will be led through a teacher-paced Nearpod presentation on le passé compose. The presentation will give a short lesson and the allow students to practice and apply what they have learned.

Space Race

BYOD

Divide students into “houses” have them participate in a Socrative space race against other houses.

Unit Goals Review

BYOD

To end the lesson, students will log into Edmodo and see the unit goals, assessments (including the culminating) and success criteria for the coming lessons.

Day 2 & 3: Helpful Resources

Activity (individual)

BYOD (or 1 Computer/student)

Direct students to a Google Doc which contains a list of links to several online quizzes and games related to le passé compose. The Google Doc will be missing some links and information, which will be addressed later in the lesson. Provide students with enough time to explore the listed links.

Activity (individual)

BYOD (or 1 Computer/student)

Lead the students in a discussion of how the links were helpful. Redirect them to the Google Doc and instruct them to complete it with resources.

Students are to explain what aspect of passé compose the resource targets and how it can be useful to their classmates.

Provide students with enough time to add to the Google Doc.

Activity (class)

BYOD (or 1 Computer/student)

Students are to explore the links shared by their classmates and rate them. As a class develop a “Best Of” list from the document.

Students will be asked to create a Pinterest board of resources.

Activity (group)

BYOD (or 1 Computer/group)

Have students gather in their houses and discuss what they still find confusing or how they want to take the language further. They will add their thoughts to an online forum in Edmodo and then give feedback comments made by other houses.

Day 4: Le passé composé avec avoir

Activity (group)

BYOD

With their devices, students will participate in a gallery walk around the classroom (or other space) with varying images posted around the room.

Each image will be on chart paper with plenty of addition room for writing.

Using the Larousse app as an aid, students will go around the room and being inspired by the image, will write related vocabulary with its accompanying transition/image/synonym/use in a sentence on to the chart paper.

Once everyone has been able to contribute to the gallery walk, the students will then go around and highlight the vocabulary that they feel is most worth learning.

Students will take pictures of each poster to use as a lexicon later in the lesson.

Activity (group)

1 Computer/group

Divide the class in to their houses. Students will watch 1-3 YouTube videos related to the themes.

Related videos are drag-and-drop activities in which they have to listen for vocabulary and le passé compose and drag the right words into the right boxes. When a house thinks that they have correctly completed the transcript, they divide in half and proceed to the next video. This process of completing a drag-and-drop listening activity, and then dividing the group in half for the next, continues until interest has peaked, or time has run out.

Activity (pairs)

Following the listening activity, and using the vocabulary and sentence structures that have been studied, students will get into pairs and write a short text based on a subject of their choice that happened in the past.

Day 5

Activity (groups)

1 Computer/group

Students will first watch the short YouTube video regarding the verbs that uses auxiliaire être. This will introduce one way of remembering which verbs go with être.

They can also refer to the Prezi introducing "la maison d'être) another way to memorize the verbs.

Have students access Stormboard where they will create simple sentences but leave the auxiliary blank. Another group will have to populate the field with either être or avoir. The short YouTube video will also serve as an example of what could be done for the culminating task.

Day 6: Review of passé composé with avoir and être

Activity (groups)

1 Computer/group

In groups, have students consolidate their knowledge and practice using passé composé in various situations. They will have to choose a few of the resources identified in lesson #2 (such as online games) to practice. They will be asked to take screenshots of their work or record (video or sound) and email the teacher, to build-in accountability, responsibility and to assess students learning (formative).

Students can also get ideas from this Prezi where they will find different activities to practice passé composé according to their multiple intelligences.

Day 7: Consolidation of passé composé with avoir and être

Activity (groups)

BYOD

Students will participate in a QR code Scavenger hunt around the school using handheld devices and QR Code Treasure Hunt Generator (http://www.classtools.net/QR/)

The students will be place in small group with one handheld device per group. They need to download a QR reader (e.g. I-Nigma | NeoReader | Kaywa) onto their mobile devices. Because the hint for the hunt are text file, the devices don't need to be connected to the internet for this activity.

Display the first intro QR on the whiteboard to get the scavenger hunt started. Students will have to find QR codes placed throughout the school, scan the code which will display a text file with a question on their device and answer it correctly. They will record their answer in a Google Doc and share it with the teacher. The students need to find the most correct answers in the allotted time. This activity with appeal to bodily/kinesthetic learners and put the emphasis on collaborative learning skills.

Day 8: Culminating activity (writing/speaking)

Activity (groups)

1 Computer/group

The students will be given 4 work periods including in-class and in computer-lab time to work on this task. The students will present their final project on the 5th period. One of the work periods will be dedicated for peer-editing. Students will put their draft script in a Google Doc and one other group will have to access the document, read their script, provide feedback and edit their draft before they can start on the final product.

Culminating Task: Students will be creating a short story using their new knowledge of the Passé Composé. Students will be given a choice of a variety of technologies that they can use to present their short story. Technologies that they can use include: Bitstrips, Powtoon, YouTube Videos, iMovie, PowerPoint, Podcast, Spotify, Audacity, e-pals, etc.. Students are welcome to suggest other technologies that will be approved by the teacher. Students will be assessed on their ability to use the Passé Composé correctly, the use of agreements, proper grammar (phrase order/prepositions), spelling and vocabulary. Finally, the students will present and share their project with the rest of the class.
