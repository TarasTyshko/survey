Duration: 7 lessons, 20 days

GRADE 11, BIOLOGY (COLLEGE)

ANATOMY OF MAMMALS

Overview

This lesson plan covers the Anatomy of Mammals unit for Grade 11 College preparation providing an understanding of the interactions of the digestive, circulatory, and repertory systems. The lesson plan integrates the effects of certain lifestyle choices, diets, and the impacts they have on the human body, organs, and systems. Using online tools, dissections, and cumulative work students will interact with the various mammalian systems and learn their purposes and interactions with each other.

Topics: mammals, respiratory, circulatory, digestive, lifestyle, diet, dissection

Instructional notes

  • Majority of student work is conducted on computers; access to a computer lab or individual computers is essential.
  • Instruct students on proper online etiquette when interacting on Wikispace Discussions
  • This lesson plan includes both a virtual and physical pig dissection.

Assessment

  • Student participation may be evaluated using online discussion and Biology in the News Discussions
  • Knowledge and Understanding assessed through the labs and quizzes.
  • Inquiry assessed through the Dissection Lab and PollDaddy activity
  • Communication assessed ongoing, through online participation, and presentations.

Students will be able to:

  • Understand the structure, function, and interactions of the circulatory, digestive, and respiratory systems
  • Identify healthy vs. affected organs
  • Identify changes in the appearance and structure of organs based on an individual’s lifestyle and diet
  • Develop skills in research, online etiquette, collaboration, critical  thinking
