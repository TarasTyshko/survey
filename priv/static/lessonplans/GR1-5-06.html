GRADE 5, Science

THE HUMAN BODY SYSTEM

Overview

This lesson plan focusses on the structures and functions of the human body systems, specifically the digestive system and its interactions. Through interactive assignments and technology, students will discover how systems in the body work together to meet our basic needs and how our choices effect our organ health.

Topics: digestive system, organ structures, functions, body systems

Instructional notes

  • This plan provides a list of supplemental activities to incorporate in to a lesson plan on the human body stems. Activities are listed in a general, flexible sequence which may be altered to meet classroom needs.

Assessment

  • Any written reports or electronic submissions can be evaluated as formative assessments as students have the ability to revise their responses (by taking additional notes or correcting inaccurate responses) for better preparation for final tests or class presentations.

Students will be able to:

  • Demonstrate a understanding of the structure and function of the human body systems and the interactions within and between systems
  • Analyze the impact of human activities and technology innovations on human health
  • Use scientific inquiry / experimentation skills to investigate change in body systems
  • Identify interrelationships between body systems

Resources Required

1 Computer/student, SMART Board

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Discussion (class)

Chart Paper

Create a KWL (What I know, what I would like to learn, what I have learned) chart of what they know so far about the body and its’ organs.

Computer work

1 Computer/student

The students will be visiting the following websites in class. These websites may be visited over a period of several days.

  • The Virtual Body: http://www.medtropolis.com/vbody.asp

Students can learn the names and functions of the organs in the digestive system, organize the digestive organs, or take a narrated tour of the stomach.

  • Body Quest: http://library.thinkquest.org/10348/

Students can tour the human body for an overview of the major body systems, stopping along the way to delve more deeply into the digestive system. Students will discover how the digestive system works and find out how each organ contributes to the functioning of the whole digestive system.

  • Human Anatomy: http://www.innerbody.com/

Students can click on Interactive Anatomy and choose one of several different anatomy systems then roll a mouse over the system to learn the names and functions of various organs. Or they can choose Anatomy Lessons to tour the human body organ-by-organ.

  • Health Trivia

Students will find fascinating facts about the human body and discover a list of truly interesting trivia about the human body. Also the student will be shown the digestive system in 3D view to gain a better understanding of the structure of the organs.

Activity (class)

SMART Board

Students will discuss the major body systems and review how those systems function. Students will brainstorm a list of things they do to keep their body functioning smoothly, like exercise. Each student will be asked to write and illustrate one health or safety rule they can follow. These rules will be displayed on the SMART Board with links to their appropriate system.

Field Trip

Ontario Science Centre

The students will be taken to Ontario Science Centre to view the movie Inside the Human Body.

Group Presentations

SMART Board, 1 Computer/group

Students will be divided in groups each representing a specific organ of the digestive system, where they will be asked to prepare a report on the function and structure of the specific system. Students must address the question: What different health problems can arise as results of their malfunction?

Each group will have to submit one report which will be presented at the end of the lesson on SMART Board which will be scored on the basis of a provided rubric. After the presentations, address any misconceptions or new topics students have learned and would like to inquire further about.

Quiz

SMART Board

A final ‘quiz’ can be given in the form of jeopardy allowing students to work collaboratively test their knowledge in a fun way.
