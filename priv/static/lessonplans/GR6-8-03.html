Duration: 8 days

GRADE 7, MUSIC/DRAMA

COMPOSING AND PERFORMING

Overview

This lesson will allow students to compose and perform through drama and music around a focused topic. Using the topic of Environmental Awareness, students will make an advertisment for their school and local community promoting sustainable and green living.

Topics: composition, performance, green living, creative process, expression, Smart Instruments

Instructional notes

  • Ensure that all students have access to an iPad to participate in this lesson plan.
  • This lesson plan heavily relies on group work â€“ ensure all groups are on task and fulling the assignment goals.

Assessment

  • The students will submit a final video presentation with video and audio that they have editing. They will have a chance to do a self-evaluation as well as a peer evaluation.

Students will be able to:

  • Apply their knowledge of music expression through the use of Smart Instruments
  • Explore technical and production abilities through iMovie and Garageband
  • Understand filming elements such as scriptwriting, casting, score-writing, set/costume design, shooting, and editing.

Resources Required

1 Computer/student, 1 iPad (minimum)/group, projector, laptop

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1 & 2: Brainstorming

Discussion (class)

Projector and laptop

Introduce the students to the final project using a video on environmental awareness and the impact of humans on the environment. The video will include an example of sound/music that was chosen to express specific emotions and moods that match appropriately with the visual presentation.

Spark a class discussion with verbal prompts such as: 

  • "How did that make you feel?"
  • "What was the big idea there?"
  • "Did you notice the music? How did the music make you feel?" 

Replay the video with no music, and then again with no visual.

Prompt the discussion again:

  • "How does this music make you feel?”
  • "What instruments were used? What types of sounds did they make?”
  • "What did you see when you heard these sounds?

Computer work (groups)

2 Computers/group

Inform students that they will be making an advertisement video for environmental awareness. Steps:

  1. In groups of 4 or 5, give students time to research different components of environmental awareness such as energy and water consumption, waste production, pollution, loss of biodiversity, and the 3 R’s (Reduce, reuse, recycle).

Students can use the following websites to research their topics:

  • http://www.ecokids.ca/pub/kids_home.cfm
  • http://pbskids.org/eekoworld/

Day 3 & 4: Scripting

Activity (group)

1-2 iPads/group, 1 Computer/group

Students are to come up with a storyline (who what where why, etc.) and outline the big idea(s) for their video using a mind map.

 

Students are to cast roles for the film production. These roles should include: actors, a director, set design (costume & props), digital or instrumental musicians, and recorder/editor.

In this part of the lesson, students are to explore their role. Provide a descriptions of the responsibilities of each role. Students may have more than one role if they choose.

 

Students are to work together to write a story board outlining the number of scenes, settings/props, and dialogue for their video. This would be good to review “green” language and environmental concepts.

Activity (group)

1-2 iPads/group, 1 Computer/group

After the script is complete and costumes and props for the set are collected, students are to begin to rehearse their lines together and begin filming scenes using an iPad and iMovie.

Each group must have at least 3 takes for each scene. Encourage self-evaluation of each take.

Day 5 & 6: Audio

Discussion & Demo (class)

Projector and iPad

Revisit the role of music in films as a form of expression. Show the students another video that displays mood-specific music.

 

Review with students how to use Garageband and hot to multi-track record.

Discuss the importance of choosing proper instrumentation. Prompt: How does the sound of the instrument make you feel? What does fast music feel like? What does slow music feel like?

Activity (group)

1-2 iPads/group

Have students record their sounds/music for the video. They are to use Garageband to record one track with a minimum of 2 different sounds and at least 8 bars long. Save this file as an mp3.

 

Students may also use the Jam Session feature for Garageband as it will allow up to 4 people to record on one session.

Day 7 & 8: Video Production

Demo

Projector and laptop

Give instructions on how to import a video, import a sound file, and edit the video and sounds to make a final product.

Demo this to the class and provide a handout on basic instructions.

Activity (group)

1 Computer/group, 1-2 iPads/group

Provide students with enough time to complete their video. This may take an additional day.

Presentations

Projector, laptop, iPads

Students will resent their advertisements and peer evaluate each other’s work.

As a class discuss what it means to be an “attentive audience” prior to presentations.
