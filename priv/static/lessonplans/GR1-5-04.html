Duration: 10 days

GRADE 4, Language Arts

Creating A Story with Comics

Overview

In this lesson plan, students learn the elements of a story, how to effectively narrative a story, and how to visually communicate their stories.

Topics: communication, character, plot, setting, climax, resolution, visualization, storytelling, media literacy

Instructional notes

  • Ensure that each student has access to Bitstrips for Schools prior to beginning the lesson.

Assessment

  • Students will conduct a self-assessment, peer-assessment, and will be assessed on their final product. The teacher will assess the students stories by assessing the characters (if the reader is able to know a lot about them), the setting (very detailed with descriptive words), problem/solution (a problem that goes well with the story), plot/events (interesting and makes sense), word choice, and conventions.

Students will be able to:

  • Create a story from beginning, middle, and end
  • Sort and classify ideas and information for their writing in a variety of ways
  • Produce pieces of published work to meet identified criteria based on the expectations
  • Identify an appropriate form to suite the specific purpose and audience for a media text they plan to create.

Resources Required

SMART Board (or Projector and laptop), various comic books, 1 Computer/student

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1

Discussion (class)

Projector, laptop

Begin reading a comic book story to the class using a projector, ensuring all students are able to read the text and see the pictures in real time. After reading the comic book ask open ended questions that the students will think/ pair/ share with their elbow partner:

  • What did you see in the story?
  • Did anything stand out to you?
  • Are there pictures in the story?
  • Were the pictures black and white or coloured?
  • Did the story have a certain order?

By having an open ended discussion in class after the story is read aloud it allows for students to share their ideas about the story and will essentially introduce the big idea of what steps are involved in making a story.

Explain that over the next two weeks students will be creating their very own stories and the final product will be a Bitstrip.

Lecture

Introduce story elements. A story is made up of 3 components: beginning, middle and end.

Ask the students questions about the previously read comic book about story elements:

  • Who are the main characters? What is the plot of the story? What is the climax of the story?

After reading the book, ask the students questions about the recap of the story, such as looking back at the comic book that we just read what was the beginning? What was the middle? What was the end?

Activity (individual)

1 comic book/student

Each student will receive a short comic book to read individually and fill out on a worksheet what the main idea was for the beginning, middle and end of the book, what was the climax, plot, and resolution, and who were the main characters? This will allow for children differentiate between each part of the story, and will allow for the teacher to see if every student has an understanding of how a story line is broken down into parts before moving on to creating their own stories.

Day 2

Demo (class)

SMART Board

Use the SMART Board to introduce a mind map using Kidspiration.

Create a mind map with the class so they understand how to create one and how to brainstorm using a mind map.

Activity (pairs)

1 Computer/pair

In pairs, have the students brainstorm ideas. The students will begin to think about a storyline and will create a mind map together with their partner.

Guide student’s work by asking the following questions: What is the main idea of your story?  Where will your story take place? What characters does your story have?

Students are encouraged to use the website Kidspiration to create a visual mind map. During their brainstorming process, they can ask their peers or teacher for feedback and ideas.

Prompt: Have the students create categories of “Who”, “Where”, “What happens”, and “When” on their mind maps and fill these in.

Day 3

Activity (class)

SMART Board

Demonstrate on the SMART Board how to use their mind map to create a rough draft for their comic strip story. Use the mind map created with the class from the previous day and turn it into a story with the class.

Ask the students to help develop a story line from this mind map and ask the students questions throughout the process.

Computer Work (pairs)

1 Computer/pair

Have students complete their mind maps if needed.

Students are to start creating their story from their mind maps in the same pairs in a word document (or in journals). Provide enough time to have students finish their stories in class or continue the following day.

Day 4 & 5

Peer editing and group work

1 Computer/pair, peer editing rubric

Go through a peer editing rubric with the class to make sure that they understand how to complete it.

After students have completed the first drafts of their stories they are to pair up with a peer group and will begin doing peer edits in class. Once they finish the peer editing, they can start their rough draft of their illustrations to go with their comic story.

Day 6

Demo (class)

SMART Board, 1 Computer/student

Use the SMART Board to show the students a video about Bitstrips and provide a step by step lesson on how to create their own Bitstrips.

As a class, turn the class story into a comic strip by using www.bitstripsforschools.com.

Show the students how to login and where to go to create their own Bitstrips. Show the students by using the class story created together to create the comic strip. Demonstrate how to create the layout of the comic strips, how to choose a character, the character’s outfit and body language, how to choose a scene, how to input different furniture and accessories into the comic, and how to create bubbles and narrator lines for the character.

If time permits, have the students explore the site and experiment with tools so they may ask questions.

Day 7 & 8

Computer work (pairs)

1 Computer/pair

The students have two periods to work in the computer lab to make their comic from the story and illustrations they have created.

Day 9

Activity (pairs)

Explain to students that they will present their comic story in a gallery walk style. Provide a choice on how students will present their story. All students will print out their story and have it on display. They can then read it to their classmates, act out their bitstrips, or any other ideas that they have.

Give the students some time to think about how they will want to present their comic strips with their partner to the class.

Day 10

Presentations

Students will present their Bitstrips to the class by doing a gallery walk.
