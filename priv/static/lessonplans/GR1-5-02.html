Duration: 1 day

GRADE 2, Social Studies

Nunavut: A look into Canada’s North

Overview

In this lesson, students will develop an understanding of Canada and its regions, learn how the environment affects people’s lives, and discover the ways in which different community needs are met. Students will learn about communities living in Nunavut through video, literature, and interactive media and compare it to their lives in Toronto.

Topics: environment, community, climate, lifestyles

Instructional notes

  • This is a single day lesson plan that can be used to supplement a unit on World Connections, geography, or Canadian history and culture.

Assessment

  • This lesson includes a formative assessment asking students to write two 1-paragraph stories.Â  Students should be able to demonstrate the differences between Inuit life in the past in comparison with the life in the present.

Students will be able to:

  • Demonstrate an understanding that the world is made up of countries, continents, and regions
  • Describe some of the similarities and differences in the ways communities around the world meet their needs
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between location and climate.

Resources Required

1 Computer/student, projector, laptop

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Discussion

Opening question to students: “Have you ever wondered what it would be like to live in Northern Canada? Think about how your life might be different if you lived in Nunavut, a Canadian territory in the far North.”

Have students do a pair share and then share their ideas about what they think might be different or similar about living in Nunavut compared to living in Southern Ontario.

Computer Work (Pairs)

1 Computer/Pair, projector, laptop, Canada map copies

Distribute paper maps of Canada to pairs of students. Instruct and assist groups to access to Google Earth and have them explore and learn how to use it.

Demonstrate the task using the LCD projector. Write places from Nunavut on a chart for students to find using Google Earth and have them mark them on their Canada Map sheet. Once students have completed the task have them come back to a common area as a group and share their findings.

Lecture

Projector, laptop

Using a PowerPoint presentation, show students then will see videos, maps and pictures that describe where and what it is like to live in Nunavut. For a resource which provides information, see the CBC archives.

Have students read a short poem that is about Nunavut.

Teach students about Toonik Tyme and the arctic games.

Discussion: Have students compare differences and similarities of Nunavut to Toronto.

Computer Work (Pairs)

1 Computer/pair (same pairs as Day 1)

Have students write two very short one paragraph stories:

  • Pretend you are an Inuit person in the past.
  • Pretend you are an Inuit person in the present.

Students should be able to demonstrate the differences between Inuit life in the past compared to life in the present. (i.e. Inuit people do not live in Igloos in the present time.)

Computer Work (Pairs)

1 Computer/pair (same pairs as Day 1)

Task: Create an income statement and balance sheet manually and then the using the accounting software. The third assignment will be handed in by the end of class in groups of two.

Homework/Presentations

Have students finish the story for homework and share it with the class during the next day
