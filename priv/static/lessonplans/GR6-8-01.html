Duration: 5 days

GRADE 6, MUSIC/MEDIA

MUSIC AND EMOTION

Overview

This lesson is designed to combine poetry, media literacy, and music to allow students to create quality multimedia presentations.

Topics: music, media studies, literature, poetry, visualization, representation, multimedia

Instructional notes

  • This plan is to be used as a supplement assignment to a media studies or music related lesson plan.
  • Develop a list of resources for students to find images and music for the assignment

Assessment

  • Students will be assessed on their participation in class discussions as well as the quality of their final product.

Students will be able to:

  • Identify media forms and explain how the conventions and techniques associated with them are used to create meaning
  • Identify useful strategies for making sense of media texts
  • Develop technical skills.

Resources Required

1 Computer/student, music player, projector, laptop, speakers

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1

Discussion (class)

Music player

Lead discussion on poetry and music. Suggested introduction: "I will now read you something, I'd like you to close your eyes, and while I read think of the images that come to mind while I read this to you".

Read the lyrics of “Imagine” by John Lennon. Discussion prompts:

  • How did this reading make you feel?
  • What did you see while I was reading this to you?
  • If you had to choose one image to describe this reading, what would it be?
  • How can we use images to convey emotion?
  • Does this reading remind you of anything?
  • Do you think this is poetry?

Play the song for the class. Ask if students had different images on their minds while listening to the song with music instead of it being read to them.

Computer activity (individual)

1 Computer/student

Introduce the topic of creating a picture montage to convey emotions. Explain to the students that they will now be developing their own photomontage with PowerPoint.

Have students view this YouTube video of a photomontage made to a song called “Where do they make Balloons” by Thy Might Be Giants.

Ask what the students like about the montage/what they think/what they would change and why.

Instruct students to print out the lyrics of a song of their choice for the next day.

Day 2

Activity (individual)

Have the students think of possible images they can use for a specific song that they chose. Have them write out a list of possible images that they will be able to use for their montage (this is just writing for now not typed the computer).

After students have compiled a list of possible images to use they will now take a closer look at their lyrics and 'pull out' words and phrases that they think are the most moving, important or meaningful to their particular song. They can highlight these words on their printed lyrics. Students will also be encouraged to consider what type of mood is created in their song and the ways that the colours and images they might use can reflect the temperament of the song.

Computer work (individual)

1 Computer/student

Show students online resources where they can access images. Show them how to save them for their montages (right clicking image, save image as, save to destination).

Students will begin creating their photo music montages.

Instruct students not to be specific at first as this will give them more images to choose from in their searches.

Provide students enough time to compile a collection of images.

Day 3

Students will begin putting the music together with images. Have them place the images in a PowerPoint presentation.

Have the students listen to the song that they will make their montage to as they create the slide so they can time their images to the music.

Day 4 & 5

Presentations

Projector, laptop, speakers

Once all students have completed their projects the teacher will assign two full class periods for presentations or spread them out throughout the week. Each student will show his or her creation to the class by playing the PowerPoint with the music playing at the same time.
