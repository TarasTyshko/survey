Duration: 5 lessons

Grade 10, Biology

Mitosis and Cancer

Overview

This lesson plan introduces the cell cycle, process of mitosis, and its importance in life and growth. Students will explore the stages of mitosis and will relate it to the development, risk factors, types, and treatments of cancer. Using computer-assisted learning tool WISE, class discussions, and a microscope lab activity, students will collaboratively engage with the cell cycle and gain visual understanding of cell differentiations, mutations and stages.

Topics: mitosis, cell cycle, interphase, prophase, anaphase, metaphase, telophase, cytokinesis, cancer, cell division,

Instructional notes

  • WISE accounts must be premade before Day 1 with specific project loaded.
  • Guide class through using WISE, logging on, navigating to activities, and ensuring all groups are on the correct page before moving on.
  • Group work is best in pairs but third member may be added. Ensure student groups have diverse levels of ability. Ensure all members of the group takes turns using the computer.

Assessment

  • Student participation may be evaluated using online discussion. Read student reflection notes on WISE and follow online discussion.
  • Knowledge and Understanding assessed through the Microscope Lab submission, Presentations and Mitosis Quiz
  • Inquiry assessed through the Microscope Lab
  • Communication assessed through the Microscope Lab, and Presentations

Students will be able to:

  • Describe cell division, stages of mitosis, and the cell cycle
  • Understand the roles of cell structures (nucleus, cell membrane, organelles)
  • Evaluate the impact of cancer on organisms through the comparison of the rate of cell division in cancerous cells and non-cancerous cells
  • Develop skills in research, microscope use and handling

Microscopes

Resources Required

SMART Board and projector, laptop with internet access, 1 computer/group with internet access, 1 microscope/student, root tip slides, Mitosis Quiz, and Peer assessment forms. Laptop will need to be connected to projector on Day 5.

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1: Introduction to Mitosis

Mitosis viewing (Class)

Computer and projector (or SMART board)

Show Mitosis video on Smart Board (click here); shows 3D mitosis occurring in single cell.

Discussion (Class)

Smart board w/projector

What did students see in the video? Drag and drop video to specific points. Ask students if they are familiar with any parts of the process/can name them. Introduce the students to the idea that mitosis occurs in cells constantly in living organisms. Briefly discuss the cell cycle. Highlight that when cells are not dividing they are in Interphase, and they enter mitosis when they are ready to divide. Record key ideas on Smart Board.

Computer Work (Groups)

1 Computer/2-3 students

In groups of 2-3 students login to WISE: Activity will begin introducing stages of mitosis (Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase, Cytokinesis) starting in interphase. Students are tasked to divide an animation in to mitosis stages and asked how they chose where stages end and the next begin. An online discussion in WISE allows the class o compare how thy chose their stages. Students are provided with definitions of 5 stages and events of each phase. Students are tasked to re-watch mitosis animation and separate it by phase.

Day 2: Mitosis Review, Introduction to Microscopes and SMART Board Group Assignment

Introduction

Smart board w/ projector

Prior to Day 2: Review student’s reflection responses from Day 1. Note areas of confusion.

Explain the agenda of Day 2 to the students. Emphasize and explain concepts that students found difficult from Day 1. Review the stages of mitosis. Give brief lesson on the parts of a microscope using a diagram resembling microscopes students will interact with on Day 3.

Computer Work (Groups)

1 Computer/2-3 students

Exercise 1: In WISE, students will review mitosis stages from Activity 1. Task: match labels with pictures of the stages of mitosis. Students are provided with mnemonic to remember stages: It’s (interphase) Pretty (prophase) Much (metaphase) All (anaphase) the Time (telophase) Copying (cytokinesis)

Exercise 2: In same groups as Day 1, students will proceed to WISE Activity 2, Parts of the Microscope. Students will be given a link to a website to learn about microscopes (show on Smart Board what activity looks like and what to do).

Task: read definitions and functions then match the labels to the diagram. Students are to show completed diagram to teacher. WISE will ask students what they would do if a microscope image is too small to see individual cells and what they will do if the image is blurry.

Work Period

Smart board w/ projector

Divide students into groups with 5 or 6 students/group depending on class size. Assign each group one stage of mitosis to present on Day 5.

Task: come up with a creative way to teach your mitosis stage to the class using the SMART Board, in conjunction with PowerPoint/paint/websites. Groups can plan their presentation on the individual computers. Groups are to be assigned times to practise with the SMART Board.

Day 3 – Microscope Lab and Introduction to Cancer

Lab (individual)

Microscopes, root tip slides, paper

Prior to Lab: Prepare root tip slides (frozen to show stages of mitosis)

Task: Using root tip slides, find cells in the different stages of mitosis. Focus the microscope to identify stages. Draw and label a diagram of a cell in each stage with a description below of what is occurring within the stage of mitosis. Hand in once complete.

Discussion: Cancer (class)

Discuss cancer with the class. Begin with what do they know about cancer? What kinds of cancer are there? What does cancer mean? How is cancer treated? Lead discussion toward cancer at a cellular level. Uncontrollable cell division, i.e. cells constantly undergoing mitosis without pausing in interphase.

Cancer viewing

Smart board w/ projector

Show video of cancerous breast cells dividing on Smart Board; Ask: what looks different compared to the video of regular mitosis. Show images of tumours in several body parts. Ask students to connect the constant cell division to the formation of a tumour.

Day 4 - Mitosis and Cancer

Computer Work (group)

1 Computer/2-3 students

Task: Plants that Affect Mitosis - in the same pairs/groups investigate 3 fictional plants that have different effects on mitosis. Determine if these plants could be used as cancer treatment. Activity will end with an online discussion where students will justify why they chose a certain plant as a cancer treatment by using the information learned about the stages of mitosis.

Work Period

The rest of the period will be for work on group presentations.

Day 5 – Class Presentations and Mitosis Quiz

Presentations (class)

Smart board w/ projector and

laptop with internet access,

Peer assessment forms

Task: Groups will present their stage of mitosis to the class beginning with Prophase group, teaching the other students what happens in that distinct phase. Students will use the SMART board in conjunction with whatever they have designed on paint/PowerPoint to aid in their explanation. Peer assessment forms will be provided to the other students, with guided comment sections. These will be used for formative assessment and not graded.

Mitosis Quiz

Students will complete a short quiz on mitosis.
