Duration: 2 days

GRADE 1, Math

Data management

Overview

In this lesson students will be introduced to data management through pictographs. Students will review how to sort and organize data before formally being introduced to the terms of data management and pictographs.

Topics: data management, sorting, primary data, patterning

Instructional notes

  • Review with students how to properly interact with the SMART board before beginning the lesson
  • Carefully monitor student pairs while on the computer to ensure they are staying on task with their partners

Assessment

  • Since this is an introductory lesson, there would not be a summative assessment, but there are formative assessments, simply by observing and asking the students questions. By observing and assessing the students while they are interacting with the SMART Board, they will be guided in the right direction by me and allowed to revise and fix their work. This will also allow for an informal peer-review.

Students will be able to:

  • Organize objects into categories by sorting and classifying objects using one attribute
  • Read primary data presented in graphs and pictographs
  • Describe data using comparative language
  • Successfully create tally charts and pictographs
  • Make inferences about data

Resources Required

SMART Board, 1 Computer/2 students, Lego pieces

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1: Categorizing Objects

Activity (class)

Coloured Lego

Instructor will begin lesson reviewing how to sort objects. Using Lego, display to students how to sort Lego pieces by shape, size, and colour.

Activity (pairs)

SMART Board

Using a SMART board, have the students will sort seasons, clothes, animals, etc. in partners. Ensure these are prepared prior to the lesson.

Day 2: Pictographs

Lecture

Explain to students that data, like objects, can be sorted into groups and categories and then graphed on a pictograph.

Review sorting and make explicit connections from sort to pictographs.

Activity (class)

SMART Board

Ask the students a question on a topic they all love. For example: “What is your favorite pizza toping?”

Tall the responses on the SMART Board and then create a pictograph using the data.

Have students record the completed pictograph and tally chart in their math booklets.

Activity (pairs)

1 Computer/pair

Have students complete activities on data management using www.starfall.com and www.mathisfun.com.
