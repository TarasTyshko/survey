Duration: 5 lessons

GRADE 11, MATH/ACCOUNTING

FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING FUNDAMENTALS

Overview

This lesson will allow students to gain hands on experience with accounting software and apply accounting practices in a computerized environment. It reinforces and integrates the knowledge and understanding of the accounting cycle.

Topics:QuickBooks, accounting cycle, finance, accounting, business, department financing

Instructional notes

  • Prior student knowledge and understanding of the accounting concepts are very important to apply to QuickBooks.
  • This lesson is to be the lab component “computer applications in accounting” of a larger unit

Assessment

  • An assignment will be handed out for the students to complete by the end of the period or by the end of the next class. To assess the students the teacher will observe the participation of each student in the class and how effectively they use their class time to finish the assignments. Each assignment will be submitted, marked for errors, and returned to the students the next day at the beginning of class.

Students will be able to:

  • Understand the basics of using accounting software
  • Recognize procedures and icons used in QuickBooks
  • Understand how to record transactions
  • Understand how transactions occur and the application of the debit and credit theory
  • Create a trial balance, income statement and balance sheet

Resources Required

Computer lab (or 1 Computer/student), access to QuickBooks, projector or SMART board

Lesson Plan

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1

Lecture/Demo

Projector or SMART Board

The students will brainstorm and create a list of why we would use computerized accounting. Students will also discuss the possible problems which may arise in using or implementing computerized accounting.

Introduce QuickBooks and relate it to the accounting cycles. The students will become familiar with software icons and where to input certain items and journal entries. Students will learn how to create accounts as well.

Computer Work (Pairs)

1 Computer/Pair

Task: Students are to journalize entries for hypothetical businesses, for example, create three entries per department. The departments would be marketing, accounting, and human resources. Each student will complete the assignment and hand it in at the end of class.

Day 2

Review

A review will be done at the beginning of class on how to do journal entries in QuickBooks.

Computer Work (Pairs)

1 Computer/pair (same pairs as Day 1)

A new assignment will be handed out to the students to work on during class time with their partners from the previous day. The assignment will consist of entering four new entries for each department and then review each posting (which is automatically done by the software program). Each group will have a chance to create new accounts for some entries and conduct a trial balance. This assignment will also be handed in at the end of the class.

Day 3

Discussion (Class)

SMART Board or projector

Summarization of yesterday’s activities, the trial balance and previous journal entries. Discuss with the students what the next steps should be in the accounting cycle to enter into QuickBooks.

Computer Work (Pairs)

1 Computer/pair (same pairs as Day 1)

Task: Create an income statement and balance sheet manually and then the using the accounting software. The third assignment will be handed in by the end of class in groups of two.

Day 4 & 5

Work Period (Pairs)

1 Computer/pair

A final assignment will be handed out for students to complete in groups of two chosen by the teacher. They will have two classes to work on a complex set of questions to apply what they have learned in QuickBooks over the past few classes. This assignment will be to answer

Questions and enter journal entries, do postings, and create financial statements for a company using accounting software. It will be submitted to the teacher at the end of day 5 in the form of a report.
