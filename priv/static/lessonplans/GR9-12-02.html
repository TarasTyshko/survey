Duration: 13 lessons

Grade 10, HISTORY

World War II

Overview

This lesson plan aims to give students an opportunity to understand the various perspectives that emerged throughout World War II. Students will develop an understanding of how different perspectives will influence a person’s perception of the information through developing cultural profiles using internet research and discussing policy through a mock town-hall meeting.

Topics: WWII, Canadian history, Wikispaces, Treaty of Versailles, Holocaust, Appeasement

Instructional notes

  • Some lessons will take place in the library/computer lab

Assessment

  • Students will be required to fill out a Wiki Page with teacher-guided questions. Each group will be responsible for the research and will be graded by the teacher on the quality of their research.
  • Students will complete several technology-designed journals that will be written from the perspective of the bio they were given at the beginning of the unit.
  • Final assignment will be devoted to a Town Hall meeting, where the various groups will be brought together. The groups’ performance to the specifications of their role will be assessed for accuracy, analysis, evaluation, and creativity.

Students will be able to:

  • Analyze the development of French-English relations in Canada, with reference to key individuals, issues, and events
  • Assess Canada’s participation in war and contributions to peacekeeping and security
  • Understand how to record transactions
  • Organize and record information gathered through research
  • Identify different viewpoints and explicit biases while interpreting information for research
  • Express ideas, arguments, and conclusions, as appropriate for the audience and purpose, using a variety of styles and forms

Resources Required

Computer lab, library access.

Lesson Plan

Lesson Preparation:

At the beginning of the unit, students will be given a passport with a person’s bio from the point in time. As the class goes through the unit, students will have to write three separate journal entries from their person’s perspective. These students will be grouped together into one of six groups that have similar perspectives (See Chart 1.1). Within the groups, students will be responsible for completing research through the Internet along with being able to use class resources. A Wiki Page will be used in order for the group to enter their research. The page will also update the class and teacher of all the information they have gathered, which will allow for a collaborative learning environment. Each Wiki will have a set of guided questions that will need to be answered, which will help students in guiding their research. These questions will help guide the students to the historical information that should be included in their journals. As well it will help draw the connections to the historical context. Students will have to use information from class as we go through the different topics as well as their research they find on their own. The final aspect will connect all of the various perspectives through a town hall meeting. As a group, students will decide which one of their perspectives they want to take on. In the town hall meeting they will have a discussion the conscription crisis. Each group will be expected to choose one perspective and argue from that point of view.

Schedule

Activity & Tools

Tasks

Day 1: Introduction to World War II

Hook (in-class)

Computer with LCD projector, whiteboard

Lesson will begin with a think/pair/share activity, which will focus on a discussion on what students know about war. This will enable the students to access their prior knowledge and for the teacher to diagnostically assess the students. During the class discussion the teacher can write down some of the student’s thoughts about war on the white board. The teacher can address the points raised, either correcting the misconceptions or supporting accurate statements. This hook will lead into the introduction of the new unit, World War II.

The teacher will than hand out a worksheet, “Key People, Places, Ideas” which gives the students a brief overview of the unit that the students will be involved in. Students will be given a chance to look the sheet over in partners and then ask the teacher any questions that may arise. The last part of the class, the teacher will introduce the summative task the students. Students will be shown the different groups and roles that will be able to represent within the project. The teacher will assign the roles to the students, which will allow for differentiated grouping to allow for the best mix of students. With the remaining time, students will get into their groups and have time to discuss the assignment.

Day 2: Introduction to World War II (Cont.)

Lecture

Worksheet

Lecture on the Treaty of Versailles and Adolf Hitler. Students will be given a worksheet to complete, as they learn about the different ways in which the Treaty of Versailles led to the anger of the German people. The main focus will be on whether or not the Treaty was a fair agreement to the German people. The Treaty will provide a lead into Adolf Hitler’s beliefs. The class will deconstruct quotes from Adolf Hitler, which will allow them to gain an understanding of Hitler’s beliefs. The main goal will be for students to understand Hitler’s perspective and to gain an understanding of why he became who he was

Day 3: Technology and Steps/Causes of WWII

Computer Work (groups)

Computer lab

Introduce different technologies students can use for their journals: Prezi, Glogster, PowerPoint, Bit Strips, and video diaries.

Students are to be given a group Wiki Page, which they will be using in order to guide their research. The teacher will run them through the questions they are responsible for answering. Students will be given a list of websites that will help them find some of the information they will be looking for.

Each group in their wiki page will have to complete a set of questions on their group. For each group they are going to have to find out the primary concerns/issues; their specific role in the war; any stories about their role. In order to provide a historical context, each student will have to incorporate their research in to their personal journal. During the rest of the class time students will have time to begin their research as a group.

Day 4

Lecture

Lecture on the steps that led to World War II.

Computer work (groups)

Computer lab

Students will be split up into groups and will look at some of the different events that led up to World War II. The goal at the end of class will be for the students to complete a worksheet that argues whether World War II was caused because of Hitler’s Aggression or the World’s Appeasement.

Day 5

Lecture and Computer Work (individual)

Computer lab

Lecture about causes of World War II. Students will take notes through a graphic organizer, which will help guide their notes.

At this point they will be given the rest of the class to work in their groups and start answering the questions outlined on their Wiki Page. At the end of the class the teacher, will inform the class that by the end of the weekend they should have the majority of their research completed.

The group will be responsible for dividing up the questions and answering them.

Day 6 & 7: Events and Battles of WWII

Assignment Help

Students will be able to ask the teacher any questions about the project that may have come up as they were completing their research. The teacher will post some comments on each group’s wiki in order to help guide them in the project.

Video Viewing

Saving Private Ryan

Students will then watch the opening scene of “Saving Private Ryan”. Students will complete a “Placemat” cooperative learning activity, which will help them gain an understanding of the feelings involved in war.

Discussion (class)

SMART Board or Laptop & projector

The class will have a discussion of the turning points in the war. Through the use of Google Earth the teacher will present a tour to the students. The tour will help the students gain an understanding of the terrain the soldiers had to fight in during the battle.

For the rest of the class, students will work in groups complete the activity in through a “Jigsaw” cooperative learning structure. This activity will lead into the next day. The class will be divided into three different groups, which will focus on different battles within the war (land, air and sea). Each expert group will complete a worksheet on the specific battles they are given. The next day, students will return to their home groups and share the information on the specific type of battles they focused on within their expert groups. To conclude the lesson, each group will complete a Venn diagram on the differences and similarities of the battles.

Work Period (groups)

Worksheet

Students will complete the activity in a “Jigsaw” cooperative learning structure. This activity will lead into the next day. The class will be divided into three different groups, which will focus on different battles within the war (land, air and sea). Each expert group will complete a worksheet on the specific battles they are given. The next day, students will return to their home groups and share the information on the specific type of battles they focused on within their expert groups. To conclude the lesson, each group will complete a Venn diagram on the differences and similarities of the battles.

Day 8: The Home Front

Activity (groups)

Students will be placed in small groups (4 members), which they will rotate through four different stations throughout the class. Each group will stay at one station for 15 minutes each. The stations will ask students to complete a separate activity/task, which will further their understanding of the specific aspect of the home front during World War II. The goal of the lesson is for students to come away with a deeper understanding of the various perspectives on the home front. The first activity will focus on Japanese Canadians and internment. The group will ask students to read a passage front an interned Japanese Canadian and complete a worksheet.

The second activity, students will watch a video called, “ The Pacifists Who Went to War (http://www.nfb.ca/film/pacifist_who_went_to_war)” and completing a cubing activity.

The third activity requires the students to complete a picture deconstruction, which show women’s roles during the role.

Lastly, students will complete a mock conscription vote and have a brief reading to complete on the crisis. As students work on their activities, the teacher will walk around to make sure the students are on task and can answer any questions the student’s may have. Students are reminded that they should complete their first journal by the end of the week.

Day 9 & 10: Library Periods for Summative Assignment

Work Period (groups)

Computer lab or 1 Computer/group, or library

Students will have already formed their small groups (4 members) to work on their summative project and will use these two library periods to do research and work on their journals. These work periods will be ideally on a Monday and Tuesday in order for students to receive feedback on their rough copies, which were posted to the class website over the weekend. This will enable students to get the majority of their rough copies and final product completed in class. Allowing them to ask questions of the teacher and ensuring they have a good product. During this time the students can also work in their groups discussing which perspective they will take in the town hall meeting. The role of the teacher during these two periods is to ensure the students are on task, that they understand how to effectively use the technology and give students feedback on their journals. The teacher should also mark down what technologies are being used by the students.

Day 11: Holocaust

Lecture

Inform the students about some of the background information about the Holocaust. Students will then participate in a small class discussion about the Holocaust. This will allow any questions or myths that maybe present in the class to be addressed by the teacher.

Discussion (groups)

Holocaust photos (drawn and actual)

In groups of 4-5 students, each group will be given two pictures. One picture will show an artist’s rendering of the holocaust and the other being an actual picture from the holocaust. The group will have to compare and contrast the pictures and deconstruct both pictures to decode its message. While the students are in their groups, the teacher should walk around the class and listen in on the group discussions.

Day 12: End of the War

Work Period (pairs)

End of War worksheet

Task: Complete work sheet in pairs that will conclude the end of the war.

Documentary Viewing

Projector or Screen

Class will watch a short documentary on the end of the war, which will give them a visual representation.

Work Period (pair)

1 Computer/pair

Students will be paired up with another student from another project group. Students will pull up their work to that point. Each pair will be responsible for completing a peer assessment checklist. Students will also give constructive feedback to their peers on how to improve their product. During this time the teacher should circulate around the class and check in to see where students are at in there process. With any remaining time in the class, students will get back in their groups and discuss their plan for the Town Hall meeting

Day 13: Town Hall Meeting

Discussion (class)

The class will have an open discussion on the conscription crisis. Each group will have to respond to the issue based on their perspective while demonstrating they understand the historical context. All the groups will have an opening statement on the issue. At this point there will be a free flowing conversation about the issues. If the discussion stalls it will be the responsibility of the teacher to have a set of guided questions. The teacher will assess the students through class participation and the quality of the group’s answers and individual answers. This will allow the student’s to see the varying perspectives within the class. Overall this will provide a conclusion to the World War II unit.
